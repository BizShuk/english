# 6. Communication / Language (溝通與語言)

`Sources: Atlantis, SG1, book, random`

## punchline

`笑點;結尾妙語` (n.) `[book]`

> Everyone laughed at the punchline of his joke.

大家都因為他笑話的笑點而笑出聲來。

## tautology

`重言式;同義反覆` (n.) `[book]`

> Saying "it is what it is" is often considered a tautology.

說「它就是它」常被視為一種同義反覆。

## critique

`批評;評論` (n.) `[book]`

> The professor gave a detailed critique of my essay.

教授對我的文章給了詳細的批評。

## purport

`聲稱;意指` (v.) `[book]`

> The document purports to be an official statement.

這份文件聲稱是公司的正式聲明。

## jargon

`術語` (n.) `[random]`

> The report was full of technical jargon.

報告充滿了技術術語。

## discourse

`論述` (n.) `[random]`

> The professor delivered a thoughtful discourse on ethics.

教授就倫理學發表了深思熟慮的論述。

## parlance

`說法;用語` (n.) `[SG1][random]`

> In legal parlance, this is called "negligence."

在法律用語中,這稱為「過失」。

## sarcasm

`諷刺;挖苦` (n.) `[SG1]`

> His sarcasm was lost on the serious commander.

他的諷刺在那位嚴肅的指揮官面前完全不起作用。

## testimony

`證詞;見證` (n.) `[unknown]`

> The witness gave her testimony in court.

證人在法庭上給出了她的證詞。

## luminous

`發光的;明亮的` (adj.) `[SG1]`

> The luminous stars filled the night sky.

明亮的星星佈滿了夜空。
