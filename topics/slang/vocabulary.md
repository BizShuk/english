# 19. Slang / Casual English (俚語與日常)

`Sources: bodyhealth, taiwan, movie, manager`

## riffraff

`三教九流;雜魚` (n.) `[bodyhealth]`

> The store hired extra security to keep out the riffraff during the sale.

商店在特賣期間雇用額外保全以擋下閒雜人等。

## cesspool

`污水坑;藏汙納垢之處` (n.) `[bodyhealth]`

> The abandoned factory became a cesspool of contamination.

那座廢棄工廠成了污染的藏汙納垢之地。

## morgue

`停屍間;太平間` (n.) `[bodyhealth]`

> The body was taken to the morgue for identification.

遺體被送到太平間進行身分確認。

## spunk

`勇氣;膽量` (n.) `[bodyhealth]`

> It takes spunk to try a new facial treatment for the first time.

第一次嘗試新的臉部療程需要勇氣。

## disavow

`否認;撇清關係` (v.) `[bodyhealth]`

> The company had to disavow the fake rumors about its products.

該公司必須否認關於其產品的虛假謠言。

## get smoked

`被擊敗;慘敗` (phr.) `[bodyhealth][movie]`

> Our team got smoked in the championship game last night.

我們的隊伍在昨晚的冠軍賽中慘敗。

## posterize

`強力灌籃;強力擊敗` (v.) `[movie]`

> Kong managed to posterize his opponent with a single powerful punch.

金剛用一記強而有力的拳頭成功擊敗對手。

## testosterone

`睪固酮;男子氣概` (n.) `[bodyhealth][movie]`

> The film is full of testosterone and explosive action scenes.

這部電影充滿了男子氣概和爆炸性的動作場面。

## comfy

`舒適的` (adj.) `[taiwan]`

> This sofa is so comfy that I could nap here all day.

這張沙發好舒服,我可以整天在這裡打盹。

## double feeling

`矛盾的心情` (n.) `[taiwan]`

> I have a double feeling about moving abroad for a new job.

對於出國從事新工作,我的心情很矛盾。

## pretentious

`做作的;裝腔作勢的` (adj.) `[taiwan]`

> The restaurant felt pretentious, with tiny portions and high prices.

那家餐廳感覺很做作,份量小但價格昂貴。

## nitpick

`吹毛求疵` (v.) `[taiwan]`

> Please stop nitpicking every small detail in my report.

請不要再對我報告中的每個小細節吹毛求疵。

## nitpicker

`吹毛求疵的人` (n.) `[taiwan]`

> Don't be such a nitpicker.

不要那麼吹毛求疵。

## toil

`辛勤工作;勞動` (v.) `[taiwan]`

> They toiled in the fields from sunrise to sunset.

他們從日出到日落都在田裡辛勤工作。

## nitty-gritty

`細節;核心` (n.) `[movie]`

> Let's get down to the nitty-gritty of the project plan.

讓我們進入專案計畫的核心細節。

## vernacular

`方言;白話` (n.) `[movie]`

> The novel is written in local vernacular.

這部小說是用當地方言寫成的。

## oddball

`古怪的人;怪咖` (n.) `[movie]`

> He's a bit of an oddball, but very talented.

他有點古怪,但非常有才華。
